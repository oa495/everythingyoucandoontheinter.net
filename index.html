Extending Poetry Through Computation

This site was created by Omayeli Arenyeka for her workshop Extending Poetry Through Computation at the School Of Poetic Computation. It was previously taught at IF/THEN.

The idea is that "Words, phrases, sentences and lines have their own tactility and affordances." These affordances, or invitations can translate to computational qualities. There can be areas in a poem or a piece of text that welcome touch or, where the poem jumps off the page or goes on forever: computational openings.

In this workshop, participants close read poems to look for computational openings: places where the poem may invite interaction/touch, where it could move or grow, where it could curl up, places that map to the possibilities inherent in computation. And work on extending their own poems through computation.
